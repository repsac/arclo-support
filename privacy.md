# Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** June 16, 2026

## What Arclo does with your data

**Nothing.** Arclo is an on-device game. It does not collect, store, or transmit personal data. There is no user account, no analytics backend, no tracking, and no advertising.

## Data stored on your device

Arclo saves a small amount of gameplay data locally:

- Your current level for each mode and tier
- Best scores and best level times
- Your full-game unlock status
- App preferences (sound and music settings, Orbit orientation, and similar)

All of this stays on your device unless you use the optional iCloud sync described below.

## iCloud sync (optional)

If you are signed in to iCloud, Arclo synchronizes the small set of data above across your Apple devices using Apple's key-value storage. This keeps your level progress, best scores, best times, and unlock in step everywhere.

- Sync is handled entirely by Apple. Arclo does not operate any server.
- The synced data is limited to game progress, scores, times, preferences, and unlock status. No personal information, no usage analytics, no identifiers.
- If you are not signed in to iCloud, everything still works and is saved only on your device.
- Apple's privacy policy governs iCloud: <https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/>

## Game Center (optional)

If you are signed in to Game Center, your best scores are submitted to leaderboards so you can compare runs with other players.

- This is managed by Apple's Game Center. Arclo only submits a numeric score to the relevant board.
- If you are not signed in to Game Center, the rest of the game works normally and no scores are submitted.
- Apple's privacy policy governs Game Center: <https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/>

## App Store purchase

The one-time full-game unlock is processed entirely by Apple's App Store. Arclo never sees your payment information. Your purchase is associated with your Apple ID for restore purposes.

## Microphone, camera, location, contacts

Arclo does not request or use the microphone, camera, location, contacts, or any other personal device data.

## Crash reports and diagnostics

Arclo does not include any third-party crash reporter, analytics SDK, or tracking library. If you choose to share crash logs with Apple via **iOS Settings > Privacy & Security > Analytics & Improvements**, Apple may include Arclo crash data in the reports they send to developers. This is controlled entirely by you through iOS's system settings.

## Children's privacy

Arclo does not knowingly collect data from any user, including children.

## Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes in a material way, the update will be reflected here and noted in the app's release notes.
